Jammu To Hyderabad

     -Contributed by Abhinandan, Vikas and Debi

The trip started when two of us left Hyderabad by road towards Jammu on 24th November. I started by flight for Delhi on 25th joining the two in Noida on the evening of 25th. We drove till Panipat and stayed at a close friends place. We were welcomed and had sumptuous food. In this area radish are very tasty unlike the highly pungent ones that you get in Huderabad. The Delhi to Chandigarh road had some construction going on and thus this stretch of road was bit cluttered. We left Panipet early morning for Jammu. On the way, we stopped in Kurukshetra and had breakfast at our relatives' place. We had aloo and paneer paranthas with white butter and pickled radishes, cauliflower and carrots. Due to lower temperatures in these areas pickle can survive mild natural preservatives of masala and oil, and thus they are more like vegetables compared to typical Indian achar. By evening we have reached Katra, Jammu. The road overall is in good condition. At Jammu border, we had to give the COVID declaration, we stayed at Lemon Tree in Katra, Jammu.

Next day 27th November we started for Vaishno Devi trek. There were not many people and the views were great. There is a cable car from Bhavan to Bhairovnath temple (the peak). We had awesome curry-rice and desi ghee ka halwa at the peak. There were many semi-domesticated monkeys and langoors mingling with humans for food. You get such sights in countries like India where animals have figured out how to live with humans.

We were back early evening. Went for a walk around the town bought some mushrooms and almonds from Chunni Lal's shop. Due to lack of sale the even the famous Chunni Lal handed me items that were sleeping in the shop for months, due to lack of tourist visiting his shop due to Corona pandemic. We had an early dinner and were sleeping by 8pm. We stayed in lemon tree and resented the vegetable salad that they served as it consisted of wafer thin slices, quite unlike what you get in a smaller roadside shop.

Next day we started for Amritsar at 4am. Had bread toast and tea at a roadside stall. By sunrise we were close to Pathankot. We reached Amritsar by breakfast time and stayed at Mariott close to Golden Temple. In breakfast we had famous Amritsari Kulcha with chola and other sumptuous delicacies.

The Golden temple was very immersive religious experience. Nobody was wearing mask and there was no fear of COVID. I was asked to remove the mask when in the queue for darshan. We were given desi ghee ka halwa after darshan. We had lunch at Kesar ka Dhabba, a small non descript place, I found it average. Afternoon we had few drinks and snoozed off early.

Next day we were again on road early ! This time on way to Bikaner. For me it was my first trip to Rajasthan so I was excited to get my first glimpse of desert.

At Bikaner we stayed at the royal Lallgarh Palace Fort. The best part of the stay was to walk around the fort and get a glimpse of how royalties lived in the olden days. Many of the rooms are preserved with old decor, carpets and furniture.

We had Lalmaas and Sangri in the dinner served to us royally. It was one of the most awesome food we had on our stay anywhere !

We decided to leave after sunrise as the drive to Jaiselmer is very scenic and we do not want to miss any of it by starting in the dark !

The route was indeed scenic, we stopped on the way to buy beer.

We stayed at Mariott, Jaiselmer. It was nice and big. Spend one day relaxing. We had local delicacies like Gatte ki sabzi and Rajasthani papad etc. Next day we were headed for Bombay at 3am. This was our earliest start ever. The plan was to cover 1k kilometers to reach outskirts of Bombay. That day we did cover more than 1k Kms passed from Rajasthan to Gujarat to Maharastra. In Gujarat stopped on way for Dhokla and tea. The food changed to bada pav and lassi by evening. The road was full of hotels and resorts and there concentration increased as we closed in on Bombay. At about 7pm we reached New Bombay and decided to walk-in into a decent hotel just off the main route. We had dinner and next day we were not in a rush to drive. Home, in Hyderabad was just 800Kms away. Next day we passed thru Maharastra, Karnataka and finally into Telangana. The day started with having typical Maharastrian breakfast of Misal Pav and tea and ended with having home cooked meal.
