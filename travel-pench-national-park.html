Pench National Park

     -Contributed by Abhinandan Prateek and Vikas Gaur

An impeccable place to walk down the memory lanes of your childhood days of the famous story The Jungle Book. In the heart of India, Madhya Pradesh, Pench National Park is located in the districts of Seoni and Chhindwara. Named after the pristine River Pence which flows through the park, Pench National Park is one of the most popular wildlife reserves in India. The park also has its mention in the famous story of 1894, ‘The Jungle Book’, penned down by the renowned English author Rudyard Kipling. The story was worldwide applauded, and hence, the national park became one of the popular national parks in the world in the 1970s and also a prominent tourist attraction in India. Since then, every year oodles of wildlife buffs flock to this place just to spot the famous characters of the book, Akela (The Indian Wolf), Raksha (the female Wolf), Baloo (Sloth Bear), and the vicious Shere Khan (Royal Bengal Tiger).



Sprawls over a massive area of 758 sq km, including the core area, Pench National Park has rich wildlife which proffers visitors an opportunity to indulge into one of the finest wildlife experiences in India. Since its inception in 1965 as a wildlife sanctuary, the park serves as the cosy nest of numerous wild creatures including the Royal Bengal tiger, jackal, peafowl, wild dog, wild boar, sloth bear, Indian leopard, fox, striped hyena, monkey, gaur, barking deer, four-horned antelope, Indian wolf, and more such enthralling species. However, later in the year 1975 and 1992, the sanctuary got the recognition of national park and tiger reserve, respectively.



We started from Hyderabad at about 6:30. The sky was clear and wheather pleasant. There was no traffic on the roads while we drove out of Hyderabad onto the highways. Roads were extremely good. There were some checks on state border specially for drink driving. In case you are carrying alcohol bottles across states it is better to get a permit from the place you made purchase. That's what we were told, the rule is to extort money and most of the people caught will not even know about this and will end up paying bribes just for having a bottle or two with them. You have been warned !

Google map estimated that we will reach Pench in about 8 hours, we did reach there about 40mins early. By 3:30pm we were in the resort checking in.

A sumptuous meal was served and we ate to our heart's content!

An evening walk to the Kohka lake. The lake is located around 5 kms from the resort. The manager at the resort told us that he could organize a jeep to take us there. We preferred to walk. We strolled towards the lake in the evening. There were fields on both sides and beautiful birds perched on trees and electricity lines. We walked while clicking pictures of the sunset. The lake is huge and serene. We walked on the lakeshore and did kayaking. It was a sublime experience with darkness setting in and quiet deep water all around us. While coming back it turned dark. We were carrying a torch and that was helpful. On the way back we clicked some moonrise pictures.

Next morning we were given a courtesy wake-up call at 4am ! We were given an express breakfast of bread toast and ginger tea. We also got some food packed. In the hindsight packed food was not required as we hardly ate any. I was carrying sony nex 5 with the wide lens. This is in month of November and in the morning it was very cold, could have done better with a jacket.

Everyone around was interested in watching the tiger. The first time around we got a glimpse of it hidden in a bush. We were disappointed as that cannot be called a sighting. While we were driving away the safari guide got the signal that the tiger has moved. We rushed back, same as many others on that safari. And voila ! we watched the tigeress Langdi walked past us in full glory. That was one successful trip.

In the evening we asked for Mahua, a local white wine that is made out of Madhuca longifolia flowers. The restaurant staff who was from local village got us a bottle of this scented liquor in a Bisleri water bottle. The liquor had the flowery smell of the Mahua flower, the initial taste was bit strong somewhere like goan Feni. But when you focus on the delicate aroma and let the taste settle in you get the fruity/flowery after taste. Overall a recuperating drink after day long activity, no affect was felt next morning. Needless to say, a superb drink with the smell of Mahua flower and effect of vodka.

Next day we were ready to drive back. We were given a quick custom breakfast by courteous MP Tourism resort staff. The nice guys at the resort packed food for us too. Paranthas with Methi Aloo tasted so good on our way back, highly recommended.
